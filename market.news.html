Hong Kong Passes Stablecoin Licensing Bill

On May 21, 2025, Hong Kong’s Legislative Council passed a new bill establishing a licensing regime for fiat-backed stablecoin issuers. Any issuer operating in Hong Kong or issuing stablecoins backed by HKD must obtain a license from the Hong Kong Monetary Authority. The law sets strict requirements for reserve management, redemption procedures, and risk oversight to protect public interest and investors. Source: reuters.com

2. Morgan Stanley Turns Bullish on U.S. Stocks

Morgan Stanley revised its outlook, claiming the market bottom is in. The firm predicts the S&P 500 will reach 6,500 by mid-2026, with a bullish case of 7,200. Key reasons include anticipated Fed rate cuts, a weakening dollar, and gains in productivity driven by AI. Source: marketwatch.com

3. CATL’s Successful IPO in Hong Kong

Chinese battery giant CATL had a strong debut in Hong Kong, with shares rising 16% on the first day. The company opted for Hong Kong over the U.S. due to political tensions. Major backers include Kuwait’s sovereign wealth fund and Oaktree Capital. The move signals Hong Kong’s rising role in global digital asset markets. Source: ft.com
